Diffusion-based vision-language-action (VLA) policies perform well on robotic manipulation, but per-decision reasoning remains opaque. To our knowledge, we present the first per-step, per-modality application of Integrated Gradients (IG) to a diffusion-policy VLA. The method attributes every control decision of the Robotics Diffusion Transformer (RDT) to vision, language, and state inputs across ManiSkill3 episodes. It uses a deterministic Gaussian log-density target through a fixed-seed denoising chain per policy call. We evaluate on four tasks with two seeds, covering 1,341 signal-bearing decisions among ~1,900 policy calls. Median completeness error is 1.9 to 2.5% on vision and language. Vision insertion and deletion AUCs of 0.79 to 0.87 and 0.29 to 0.35 pass pre-specified faithfulness bars. Language AUCs mostly miss their bars at 170M, and RDT-1B language insertion is seed-sensitive, passing at 2 of 3 seeds. We report two structural findings. First, the log-density change under deletion does not track policy competence. A fine-tuned RDT-1B improves vision AUCs at all three seeds (pooled over all calls, insertion 0.940, deletion 0.288) while its vision deletion magnitude stays near -0.00015 nats. A direct measurement traces this to the per-entry quadratic log-density readout, not the denoiser holding the action fixed. Ranking-based metrics, not magnitudes, are the right faithfulness tool. Second, model-randomization sanity correlations persist under frozen-target and full-backbone randomization. This follows from IG's input-anchored form, while input randomization remains discriminating and passes (rho <= 0.17 on vision). A raw L2 target restores a usable deletion magnitude of -1.23 at k=5% in action-space units, against -0.0014 nats for the log-density target on the same population, with AUCs faithful at a measured completeness cost. We release the pipeline code and metrics records.
